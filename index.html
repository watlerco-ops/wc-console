How to read this

Free cash, not bank balance. The bank balance is a mix of your money and other people's money. Free cash is what is left after customer deposits, vendor bills, cards, the floor you never cross, and the tax you owe but have not set aside. Distribute against free cash, never against the balance.

Held deposit money is the number that moves everything. It is collected cash minus earned revenue on open jobs. When it runs larger than checking, the deposit money has already been spent, which means the next job's deposit is funding the last job's work. That is the read to fix first, and it is fixed by finishing work, not by billing faster.

Draft invoices are not billings. JobTread generates the whole payment schedule as drafts the moment an order is approved. Counting them as billed inflates over-billing dramatically. This console counts approved and pending invoices only.

Everything job-level is approved documents and actuals, never budgets. Contract is approved orders. Billed is issued invoices. Cost is JobTread actuals, voided bills excluded. Earned is actual cost grossed up at the blended margin, capped at contract, because per-job cost columns are not yet trustworthy as a denominator. Three independent reads put the margin at 29.0 sold, 30.3 realized on finished jobs, 27.6 on year-to-date cash flow, so 29 is the default; QuickBooks reads 19.1 only because about $156K of job cost bypassed JobTread.

QuickBooks supplies the cash side only. Bank balances, cards, the line of credit, and net income live nowhere else, so they come from QuickBooks. Every job-economics number comes from JobTread, and the flags above are the running reconciliation between the two.
